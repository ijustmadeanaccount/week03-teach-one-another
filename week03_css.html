Style Descriptions

Style 1:
Style 1 is a dark, gray background with a predominantly yellow and orange foreground. I think one of the most clever thing I did is that I made the list on top change its direction from top-to-bottom to left-to-right--taking advantage of the display property allowed me to do this.
Contributors: Zach Delano

Style 2:
Style 2 plays around with text-decoration (such as overline) and aligning of text. It also focuses more on id-specific styling. A lot of the color scheme is tomato, cyan, and black.
Contributors: Joseph Clark

Style 3:
Style 3 has more of a neutral color theme. There are some dark red, light green, and dark blue colors. I played around with text-transform and text-shadow with this theme. It was cool to see the shadows working in action, and I didn't quite have a solid understanding of text-transform before.
Contributors: Zach Delano

Style 4:
Style 4 is a more of a teal/turquoise theme. One cool thing about this is that when you hover over the headers they become underlined. I didn't know you could apply hover for almost anything. I applied this knowledge to paragraph elements as well--they get a darker background when you hover over them.
Contributors: Zach Delano
